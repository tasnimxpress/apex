📖 Start Here — What the Terms Mean
Plain-English guide to every word used on this dashboard. No finance background needed.
AUM (Assets Under Management)
The total money we currently manage for live customers — the size of the business.
Net Flow
Money customers paid in, minus money they took out. What actually stayed with us.
Churn
The share of accounts that have closed or been stopped. Lower is better.
SIP
A plan where a customer invests a fixed amount every month — like a recurring deposit.
SIP Persistency
Of the monthly payments a customer was meant to make, how many they actually made. Higher = more committed.
Survival (12-month / 24-month / 36-month)
How many customers are still active 1, 2, or 3 years after they joined.
Cohort
A group of customers who all joined in the same year, then followed over time.
Book Multiple
Money in the fund now ÷ money customers originally put in. Above 1.0× = the fund grew their money; below = it shrank.
Composite Score (0–100)
One overall quality grade per fund, blending all the measures above. Higher = better.
Customer Segment
Look-alike groups of customers the computer sorted automatically (by age, balance, behaviour).
Cross-sell
Selling an extra product to a customer we already have — cheaper than finding new ones.
Relationship Manager
The staff member who looks after a customer's account.
Crore (Cr)
A South-Asian number unit = 10 million. So ৳1 Cr = ৳1,00,00,000 (ten million taka).
Surrender
When a customer cashes out / withdraws their investment.

Cross-sell Opportunity Sizing
Extra yearly revenue if existing customers each buy a little more. Three ways to do it.

Sales idea 1 (sell a second fund) targets 4,251 active customers through their existing relationship manager — no cost to find them, and the most likely to say yes.
📌 Why are there two numbers? A "single-fund" customer has invested in only one of our four funds, so they're the obvious person to offer a second fund to. We count this group two ways:
• 4,251 — "active" customers: still have at least one live account today. This is the clean, ready-to-call list for the sales team.
• 4,598 — "not-yet-closed" customers: the wider pool that also includes people who haven't formally left but have gone quiet (no active account right now).
Use 4,251 for an immediate campaign; use 4,598 if you also want to win back the quiet ones. Both are correct — they just answer slightly different questions.
