Data Limitations
  • No NAV data: All AUM = book-value (capital flow) based. Investment return cannot be assessed. Book multiple measures capital retention, not investment performance.
  • SIP persistency ≠ retention: Persistency measures payment regularity. A customer can maintain 100% persistency and still close their account. Do not substitute for churn metric.
  • Customer key: Mobile phone used as unique customer identifier per case brief. Shared or changed numbers may cause 1–3% count inaccuracy.
  • RM attribution: Based on introducer_rm_name at account open. Transferred accounts retain original RM attribution — may understate current manager quality for some.
  • Churn definition (strict): Closed + Discontinued only. Inactive excluded from base calculation. Suspended (2 accounts) excluded entirely.
  • 2025 is partial year: Data through May 2025 (~5 months). All 2025 figures understate full-year totals.
Analytical Methods
  • K-means clustering (k=4): Features: AUM, installment size, age, tenor. Silhouette score 0.42. k selected via elbow method on inertia curve (WCSS).
  • Kaplan-Meier survival: Account closure = event. Active accounts censored at data cut. Cohort survival plotted at 12m / 24m / 36m milestones. Implemented via Python Lifelines.
  • Composite fund score: Net flow 40% + 36m survival 25% + churn inverse 20% + SIP persistency 15%. Normalised 0–100 within 4-fund cohort — relative, not absolute measure.
